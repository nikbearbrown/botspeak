# Portfolio Cover Memo — Template

*The first page a future reader of your portfolio sees. One page, plain language, no jargon from the book. Drafted at Chapter 13. Living document — update when your role or domain changes.*

---

## What this template is for

This Cover Memo is the single most important artifact in your finished Botspeak portfolio. It is the cover letter every future reader — a hiring manager, an admissions panel, a peer reviewer, a colleague you are trying to convince — sees first. The rest of your portfolio is the evidence. This Memo is the argument.

The Memo's job is to explain your portfolio to a reader who has never heard of *Botspeak*, has not read any of the chapters, and does not know what `CLAUDE.md` or "the Loop" or "blast radius" means. If your Memo requires the reader to have done the book's homework, it has failed.

One page. Six sections. No section longer than a short paragraph. Honest about what the portfolio shows and what it does not.

## Notes for the practitioner — these stay in the template, not the Memo

- **One page.** If the Memo runs longer than a printed page, cut. The reader is busy. Length is a credibility cost.
- **No book jargon.** Do not write "the Loop" or "blast radius" or "Three Modes" unless you have a sentence defining what you mean. The Memo is for a reader who has not read *Botspeak*.
- **Specific over generic.** "I run a four-layer verification on every piece before it ships" is stronger than "I take verification seriously."
- **Update on every job change.** The Memo is dated. A Memo from two roles ago lies to the reader about who you are now. Re-draft when you move.
- **Show, don't claim.** The Memo points the reader at the artifacts. The artifacts make the case. The Memo's job is to get the reader to the right one fast.

If you cannot write your Memo cleanly, that is information about your portfolio, not your writing. It means the portfolio does not yet have an argument it can name out loud. Go back to the artifacts. Re-read the case study. The Memo writes itself once the portfolio is coherent.
