# Botspeak: The Irreducibly Human Practitioner's Guide to AI Fluency

**Working title:** Botspeak: The Irreducibly Human Practitioner's Guide to AI Fluency
**Author:** Nik Bear Brown
**Companion volume:** *How to Speak Bot* (Brown, existing) — pattern reference companion
**Series:** First volume of the *Botspeak* series

## Audience

Recent graduate, 0–3 years into their first professional job, in a workplace where AI use is expected and unstructured. They have used at least one major chatbot at least once. They have no prior framework for thinking about AI work. They are aware their AI-assisted outputs are worse than experienced practitioners' but cannot articulate why.

The operating metaphor: an American who went to France, did not learn basic French, and is upset that the French don't understand them. This handbook is the basic French.

Motivation is competitive anxiety, not curiosity. The reader is comparing themselves to people three years more senior and losing.

## Scope

AI fluency as a teachable practitioner discipline. The book teaches a structured workflow (the Loop) and a cognitive vocabulary (the Nine Capacities, mapped onto the Three Modes, with the Seven Tiers as delegation diagnostic) that lets the reader specify, delegate, converse, discern, and diligently maintain AI-assisted work — and defend each of those decisions to a stakeholder.

The book is organized as a 14-chapter (Ch 0 + Ch 1–13) three-act arc:

- **Act 1 (Ch 0–8) — Augmentation.** Build the Loop as practitioner habit with the human in the moment.
- **Act 2 (Ch 9–10) — Automation.** Reweight the Loop for delegation without presence in bounded automation.
- **Act 3 (Ch 11–13) — Agency.** Reweight the Loop's verification and accountability work for unbounded autonomy; name the forward path.

Plus two appendices — pattern reference (cross-linked to *How to Speak Bot*) and the Seven Tiers full taxonomy.

## Voice

Feynman-flavored as defined in root `CLAUDE.md` §6. But this is a practitioner handbook, not a teaching textbook of the same kind as the finance books. The Feynman discipline applies — specification before argument, mechanism visible, jargon translated or cut, calibrated uncertainty, no authority-by-reference — but the rhetorical center is *the move the practitioner makes at the keyboard*, not *the concept the student needs to understand*. Worked examples are tasks the reader executes, not problems the reader watches the author solve.

Vendor-agnostic positioning with Claude as default examples. ChatGPT and Gemini sidebars where the move differs.

This book has no per-book voice samples in `style/` yet. Drafts produced before that folder is populated should be marked `voice-unanchored` and reviewed with that limitation in mind. Chapter 0 (the Citation That Wasn't) is the voice-setting exercise.

## Prerequisites

- The reader has used at least one major chatbot at least once.
- No prior framework. No prior reading. No technical background.
- No mathematical prerequisites beyond high-school numeracy.

## Distinguishing claim

No prior canonical practitioner handbook for AI fluency exists. *Botspeak* defines the field by giving the practitioner a workflow (the Loop), a vocabulary (the Nine Capacities), and a delegation diagnostic (the Seven Tiers), each tested on real tasks. AI literacy — what the reader has — is required. AI fluency — what the reader does not yet have — is what the book teaches.
