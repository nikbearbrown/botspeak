# Botspeak: The Irreducibly Human Practitioner's Guide to AI Fluency

**Working title:** Botspeak: The Irreducibly Human Practitioner's Guide to AI Fluency
**Author:** Nik Bear Brown
**Companion volume:** *How to Speak Bot* (Brown, existing) — pattern reference companion
**Series:** First volume of the *Botspeak* series

## Audience

A working professional, recent graduate through mid-career, whose domain identity is already formed and whose workplace expects AI use without structuring it. The reader has used at least one major chatbot at least once. They have no prior framework for thinking about AI work. They are aware their AI-assisted outputs are worse than experienced practitioners' but cannot articulate why.

The book serves three overlapping readerships:

- **Recent graduates** in an AI-augmented job, two to three years in, comparing themselves to people three years more senior and losing.
- **Mid-career professionals** with five or more years of domain expertise, encountering AI in their existing workflow, watching younger colleagues use it more fluently.
- **Graduate students** in any field whose discipline is being reshaped by AI in their lifetime.

The operating metaphor: an American who went to India, learned a little Hindi, and is upset that they cannot navigate the railway station. This handbook is the literacy-to-fluency bridge.

Motivation is competitive — domain identity intact, capability layered on. The book is **not** a re-skilling pitch. The data scientist becomes an AI-fluent data scientist. The marketing manager becomes an AI-fluent marketing manager. The journalist becomes an AI-fluent journalist. The book teaches a method the reader applies in their own field.

## Scope

AI fluency as a teachable practitioner discipline. The book teaches a structured workflow (the Loop) and a cognitive vocabulary (the Nine Capacities, mapped onto the Three Modes, with the Seven Tiers as delegation diagnostic) that lets the reader specify, delegate, converse, discern, and diligently maintain AI-assisted work — and defend each of those decisions to a stakeholder.

**The book is portfolio-driven.** Every chapter produces one or more artifacts that compose, by Ch 13, into a brand-portable portfolio the reader hands to a future employer, admissions panel, or peer reviewer. Three layers: living governing files (`CLAUDE.md`, `DESIGN.md`, `PROJECT.md` template) accreted across chapters; customized frameworks built once and refined; field-specific deliverables produced against the frameworks. The Ch 8 End-to-End Case Study is the gravitational center of the deliverables; the Ch 0 Baseline Audit pairs with the Ch 13 Final Audit as before-and-after evidence of fluency. The Cover Memo at the front of the finished portfolio is the most important single artifact — it is the cover letter every unfamiliar reader sees first.

The book is organized as a 14-chapter (Ch 0 + Ch 1–13) three-act arc:

- **Act 1 (Ch 0–8) — Augmentation.** Build the Loop as practitioner habit with the human in the moment.
- **Act 2 (Ch 9–10) — Automation.** Reweight the Loop for delegation without presence in bounded automation.
- **Act 3 (Ch 11–13) — Agency.** Reweight the Loop's verification and accountability work for unbounded autonomy; name the forward path.

Plus two appendices — pattern reference (cross-linked to *How to Speak Bot*) and the Seven Tiers full taxonomy.

## Protagonist

The book follows **Priya Banksy** — 29 years old, BA Mass Communication from Symbiosis Institute of Media & Communication (Pune), AI Content Strategist at a US digital publication on her first year of OPT. Her work spans editorial and brand content. She is the single protagonist of every case in the book; the case-setting protagonists of the earlier drafts (Diego, Maya, Aisha, Henrik, Tessa, Ash, Aya) retire — except where the lesson requires a second character in a relationship Priya alone cannot perform. The book is not the AI+1 brochure. Priya is one worked example of an AI-fluent practitioner; the reader's field is the actual subject.

Each chapter ends with an explicit translate-before-you-move-on prompt that produces the chapter's portfolio artifact in the reader's domain.

## Voice

Feynman-flavored as defined in root `CLAUDE.md` §6. But this is a practitioner handbook, not a teaching textbook of the same kind as the finance books. The Feynman discipline applies — specification before argument, mechanism visible, jargon translated or cut, calibrated uncertainty, no authority-by-reference — but the rhetorical center is *the move the practitioner makes at the keyboard*, not *the concept the student needs to understand*. Worked examples are tasks the reader executes, not problems the reader watches the author solve.

Vendor-agnostic positioning with Claude as default examples. ChatGPT and Gemini sidebars where the move differs.

This book has no per-book voice samples in `style/` yet. Chapter 0 (the Citation That Wasn't) is the voice-setting calibration — the rewritten Priya version is the active reference for all subsequent chapter revisions.

## Prerequisites

- The reader has used at least one major chatbot at least once.
- No prior framework. No prior reading. No technical background.
- No mathematical prerequisites beyond high-school numeracy.

## Distinguishing claim

No prior canonical practitioner handbook for AI fluency exists. *Botspeak* defines the field by giving the practitioner a workflow (the Loop), a vocabulary (the Nine Capacities), and a delegation diagnostic (the Seven Tiers), each tested on real tasks. AI literacy — what the reader has — is required. AI fluency — what the reader does not yet have — is what the book teaches.
