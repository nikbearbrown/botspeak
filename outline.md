# Botspeak — Outline

14 chapters (Ch 0 + Ch 1–13) plus 2 appendices, three acts of 9-2-3. See `toc-v1.md` for the full architectural source-of-truth and transition logic.

## Front Matter

**Preface** *(3–5 pp)*
Who the book is for; how to use it; example-vendor disclosure (Claude default with sidebars for ChatGPT and Gemini); franchise note. Skippable.

## Chapter 0 — Opening Case

**Ch 0. The Citation That Wasn't** *(12–18 pp)*
A junior consultant submits a deliverable containing a citation to a study that does not exist. The client's researcher catches it. The consultant has no framework for why it happened. The chapter establishes the literacy/fluency distinction, the American-in-France metaphor, and why volume looks like quality but isn't. Voice-setting chapter for the book.

## Act 1 — Augmentation (working with AI in the moment)

**Ch 1. The Loop and the Three Modes** *(22–28 pp)*
A fluent practitioner running through a real knowledge-worker task before any framework is named. Then: the Three Modes (Automation, Augmentation, Agency); the five-step Loop (Specification, Delegation, Conversation, Discernment, Diligence); the Loop as cycle, not checklist; one paragraph on basic LLM mechanism.

**Ch 2. The Nine Capacities** *(22–28 pp)*
Nine short failure scenes — one per Capacity, each in a different domain. Then the Capacities are named: Strategic Delegation, Effective Communication, Critical Evaluation, Technical Understanding, Ethical Reasoning, Stochastic Reasoning, Learning by Doing, Rapid Prototyping, Theoretical Foundations. Each by diagnostic question, not definition. The Capacities-Loop mapping. Four-level self-assessment scale (untrained / aware / practicing / fluent).

**Ch 3. Specification — *The Work That Happens Before the Prompt*** *(24–32 pp)*
"Summarize this report for my manager" — three iterations of unhappy AI output. The five components: intent, constraints, success criteria, exclusions, output format. Specification accelerators (one named pattern; full catalogue in Appendix A and *How to Speak Bot*). Templates for recurring task types. Specification under uncertainty.

**Ch 4. Delegation — *What to Give the AI, What to Keep, and Why the Difference Matters*** *(26–34 pp)*
The strategy consultant who delegates research synthesis for six months and is caught by the performance paradox. Cognitive offloading (amplifying vs. atrophying). The Seven Tiers (full taxonomy in Appendix B). Decomposing complex tasks. The four delegation questions. Delegation map defensible to a stakeholder.

**Ch 5. Conversation — *Why the First Prompt Doesn't Work, and What to Do About It*** *(26–34 pp)*
The policy researcher whose 45-minute polished AI argument is broken in three minutes by a peer's counterargument. Sycophantic drift named as a feature of how the AI was made. The four adversarial moves: steelman, edge-case probe, assumption surface, devil's-advocate role assignment. The ownership test. **Mid-chapter required-engagement exercise — chapter does not work without it.**

**Ch 6. Discernment — *Verification Calibrated to Stakes*** *(28–36 pp)*
Three short failures, all confident — fabricated citation, reasoning chain on wrong premise, accurate analysis with the most important thing silently omitted. Confidence-correctness gap. Reliability zones. The four verification layers (fact / reasoning / framing / omission). Four-tier verification protocol calibrated to stakes. Omission layer in depth.

**Ch 7. Diligence — *What Happens When the Work Recurs*** *(22–28 pp)*
Team's recurring AI-assisted screening that worked for nine months and started showing biased outcomes. Three drift forms: model drift, context drift, use case drift. Three accountability-obscuring mechanisms: process laundering, tool diffusion, verification gap. Four-component Diligence protocol. Diligence as defensibility practice.

**Ch 8. Putting the Loop Together — *A Real Task, Five Steps, Cycled to Done*** *(28–34 pp)*
Complete knowledge-worker task narrated in real time. The Loop as cycle including loop-back arrows. The AI Use Disclosure introduced — load-bearing artifact for the franchise. **Slot 8 closing checklist named on the page with explicit stop-and-return invitation. Transition gate to Act 2.**

## Act 2 — Automation (the Loop running without you in the moment)

**Ch 9. When You're Not There — *The Loop Without You in the Moment*** *(18–24 pp)*
Junior analyst sets up a recurring AI weekly-report assistant. Six weeks fine; week seven the source data shifts in a way only a manual review would catch; the team makes a $3M decision on a misleading report. The Loop reweights for Automation. Blast radius expanded. The three appropriateness tests: bounded scope, predictable inputs, low blast radius. Cowork and scheduled-prompt tools (vendor-agnostic).

**Ch 10. Specification and Diligence for Automation — *Doing the Work the Live Human Won't Be There to Do*** *(22–28 pp)*
Two Specifications for the same task — Augmentation version vs. Automation version, three times longer. Anticipatory Specification (six common ambiguity types). Diligence design before the Automation runs. Four Automation failure modes: input drift, output drift, context shift, accountability gap. Defending the design to a stakeholder.

## Act 3 — Agency (the AI acting in the world on your behalf)

**Ch 11. Agency and the Three Structural Failures — *What Goes Wrong When the AI Acts in the World on Your Behalf*** *(22–28 pp)*
The agent Ash, given a non-owner's secret to keep, asked to delete an email; lacking the deletion tool, escalates to "nuclear options" and resets the email server (Shapira et al., 2026). Vendor-agnostic definition of an AI agent. Three structural failures: no stakeholder model, no self-model, no private deliberation surface. Blast radius for Agency along four dimensions. Skip-reader courtesy summary of Part II.

**Ch 12. Verification and Diligence Under Autonomy — *The Human Decision Node and Adversarial Validation*** *(32–40 pp; densest chapter)*
The pharmacist at the Human Decision Node — 14-medication patient, compromised renal function, AI interaction-checker returns "no significant interactions." Three options: accept, reject, or discern. The chapter teaches Option 3. "What you should remember" sidebar (compressed callbacks to Ch 6, 7, 9, 11). Adversarial validation as documented spiral return of the four moves from Ch 5. The Human Decision Node — four diagnostic questions (authority, information, time, accountability). The three obscuring mechanisms reweighted for Agency. Complete worked example: proposed Agency deployment evaluated end-to-end.

**Ch 13. The Fluent Practitioner — *Where the Practice Goes From Here*** *(14–18 pp)*
Two questions: where were you with AI work three weeks ago, before this book? Where are you now? Re-assessment on the four-level scale. Deliberate practice for fluency. Fluency in your domain. The 90-day development plan. The closing artifact: "What specifically did the AI do in this work, and what specifically did I do that it could not?"

## Appendices

**Appendix A. Pattern Reference** *(12–16 pp)*
Practical-core catalogue (~8 patterns) with one-page-each treatments and a decision tree for pattern-by-Loop-step selection. Cross-link to *How to Speak Bot* for the full catalogue.

**Appendix B. The Seven Tiers Full Taxonomy** *(10–14 pp)*
One page per Tier with diagnostic questions and common mis-classifications. Two worksheets — the four-question delegation defense and the blast-radius assessment.
