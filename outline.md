# Botspeak — Outline

14 chapters (Ch 0 + Ch 1–13) plus 2 appendices, three-act arc of 9-2-3. Each chapter produces one or more portfolio artifacts the reader leaves the book holding — Layer A field-agnostic infrastructure, Layer B field-specific deliverables, tagged as deliverable / framework / diagnostic.

The book follows Priya — 29, BA Mass Communication (Symbiosis Pune), AI Content Strategist at a US digital publication, in her first year of OPT, working across editorial and brand content. Priya is the worked example. The reader's field is the actual subject. Every chapter asks the reader to translate before they move on.

---

## Portfolio spine — what the reader leaves with

Three living governing files, accreted across chapters:

- **`CLAUDE.md`** — personal coding/process constitution (built across Chs 1, 3, 4, 6)
- **`DESIGN.md`** — output/quality constitution for the reader's domain (built across Chs 6, 7, 11, 12)
- **`PROJECT.md` template** — instantiated for every significant piece of work in Layer B (introduced Ch 3, exemplified Ch 8)

Fourteen chapter-bound artifacts plus a final cover memo, organized into five sections of the finished portfolio. The Ch 8 End-to-End Case Study is the gravitational center of Layer B — earlier chapters' deliverables are smaller pieces previewing it; later chapters' frameworks were forged against it.

## Chapter 0 — Opening Case

**Ch 0. The Citation That Wasn't** *(12–18 pp)*
Priya's Tuesday morning, six months into her US publication role. A 4,200-word feature on Gen Z energy attitudes — published Friday, 11,000 shares by Sunday, retraction request from a competing publication's research editor on Monday. The load-bearing 78% Pew statistic doesn't exist. The chapter establishes the literacy/fluency distinction, the American-in-India metaphor, and why volume looks like quality but isn't. Voice-setting chapter.

> **Portfolio artifact — Baseline Audit.** Layer B with A template, diagnostic. The reader audits one of their own past AI-assisted pieces with the four-layer frame introduced informally. *Paired with the Ch 13 Final Audit for before-and-after.*

## Act 1 — Augmentation (working with AI in the moment)

**Ch 1. The Loop and the Three Modes** *(22–28 pp)*
A fluent practitioner — Priya, two years from the Ch 0 moment — running a real task before any framework is named. Then: the Three Modes (Augmentation, Automation, Agency); the five-step Loop (Specify, Delegate, Converse, Discern, Be Diligent); the Loop as cycle, not checklist; one paragraph on basic LLM mechanism.

> **Portfolio artifact — Worked Loop Log.** Layer B with A template, deliverable + log. The reader takes one recent piece of their own work through all five steps with time-stamps and notes at each move. *First piece of the keystone case study.* The reader also opens their `CLAUDE.md` here — first conventions named.

**Ch 2. The Nine Capacities** *(22–28 pp)*
Nine short failure scenes across nine domains — including but not limited to Priya. Then the Capacities are named by diagnostic question, not definition. The Capacities-Loop mapping. Four-level self-assessment scale (untrained / aware / practicing / fluent).

> **Portfolio artifact — Practitioner Profile (combined with Ch 4).** Layer A template + Layer B content, diagnostic. The capacities self-assessment lands here; the delegation map is added in Ch 4. The combined Profile is one artifact, two sections.

**Ch 3. Specification — *The Work That Happens Before the Prompt*** *(24–32 pp)*
Priya's three iterations on a single brief — "summarize this market report for the editor" — three polished prompts, three wrong outputs. The five components: intent, constraints, success criteria, exclusions, output format. Specification accelerators. Templates for recurring task types. Specification under uncertainty.

> **Portfolio artifact — Specification Library.** Layer B, deliverable. 1–3 worked, full-form specifications for the reader's typical task types — the templates they will actually reuse. Also: the reader's `PROJECT.md` template is introduced here; Priya's keystone case begins to take shape.

**Ch 4. Delegation — *What to Give the AI, What to Keep, and Why the Difference Matters*** *(26–34 pp)*
Priya's six-month delegation audit on her own work — what amplified her, what atrophied. Cognitive offloading (amplifying vs. atrophying). The Seven Tiers (full taxonomy in Appendix B). Decomposing complex tasks. The four delegation questions.

> **Portfolio artifact — Practitioner Profile (second half — Delegation Map).** Layer A template + Layer B content, diagnostic / framework. The reader's delegation map for their current work, combined with the Ch 2 capacities self-assessment into one Practitioner Profile artifact.

**Ch 5. Conversation — *Why the First Prompt Doesn't Work, and What to Do About It*** *(26–34 pp)*
Priya's 45 minutes building the case for a brand-content piece on a Series B clean-energy candidate. A senior colleague finds the cobalt-supply-chain objection in three minutes. Sycophantic drift named as a feature of how the AI was made. The four adversarial moves: steelman, edge-case probe, assumption surface, devil's-advocate role assignment. The ownership test. **Mid-chapter required-engagement exercise.**

> **Portfolio artifact — Adversarial Conversation Log.** Layer B with A template, log + deliverable. The reader runs all four moves on one piece of their own work. The log shows what was caught, what changed, what shipped. *Second piece of the keystone case study.*

**Ch 6. Discernment — *Verification Calibrated to Stakes*** *(28–36 pp)*
Three short failures in Priya's week, all confident — fabricated citation, reasoning chain on wrong premise, accurate analysis with the most important thing silently omitted. Confidence-correctness gap. Reliability zones. The four verification layers (fact / reasoning / framing / omission). Four-tier verification protocol calibrated to stakes. Omission layer in depth.

> **Portfolio artifact — Domain Verification Protocol.** Layer A template + Layer B customization, framework. Four layers, customized with field-specific checks at each. The reader's `DESIGN.md` opens here — first quality criteria named.

**Ch 7. Diligence — *What Happens When the Work Recurs*** *(22–28 pp)*
The publication's recurring AI-assisted audience-segmentation tool — built for one beat, used quietly for nine months on a different beat it was never validated for. Three drift forms: model drift, context drift, use case drift. Three accountability-obscuring mechanisms: process laundering, tool diffusion, verification gap. Four-component Diligence protocol.

> **Portfolio artifact — Diligence Framework Applied.** Layer A template + Layer B application, framework. Three-drift watch + four-component framework applied to one specific AI system the reader currently uses or owns. For self-study readers without organizational systems: a public AI tool analyzed from the outside.

**Ch 8. Putting the Loop Together — *A Real Task, Five Steps, Cycled to Done*** *(28–34 pp)*
Priya's Tuesday at 8:47 AM to 5:52 PM. Eighteen months past Ch 0. The 1:14 PM loop-back from discern to specify that defines fluency. The AI Use Disclosure introduced. **The Ch 0 / Ch 8 mirror named explicitly.** Transition gate to Act 2.

> **Portfolio artifact — End-to-End Case Study (the keystone deliverable).** Layer B with full A infrastructure, deliverable. One complete piece of the reader's work from intent through delivery, every Loop step documented, every adversarial move logged, every verification layer applied, every disclosure attached. *The gravitational center of Layer B. Every earlier chapter's deliverable is a smaller version of this; every later chapter's framework refines how this kind of work is governed.*

## Act 2 — Automation (the Loop running without you in the moment)

**Ch 9. When You're Not There — *The Loop Without You in the Moment*** *(18–24 pp)*
Priya inherits the publication's Monday-morning automated market-intel summary. Week 7, it summarizes a retracted article as if it were live news. The Loop reweights for Automation. Blast radius expanded. Three appropriateness tests: bounded scope, predictable inputs, low blast radius. Three noticing forms: sampling, adversarial tests, outcome audits.

> **Portfolio artifact — Automation Inheritance Audit + Noticing Protocol.** Layer A template + Layer B audit, diagnostic + framework. Appropriateness test applied to one inherited automation; the sampling/adversarial/outcome protocol the reader will run on it.

**Ch 10. Specification and Diligence for Automation — *Doing the Work the Live Human Won't Be There to Do*** *(22–28 pp)*
Priya specs the replacement for the inherited automation. Two specifications side by side — Augmentation version vs. Automation version, three times longer. Six common ambiguity types. Diligence design before the Automation runs. Four Automation failure modes: input drift, output drift, context shift, accountability gap. Defending the design to a stakeholder.

> **Portfolio artifact — Automation Specification.** Layer A template + Layer B content, deliverable. Fully documented spec for one automation in the reader's field, six-ambiguity-type walkthrough applied, defense memo attached. For self-study readers: a hypothetical automation the reader proposes their organization or a partner organization build.

## Act 3 — Agency (the AI acting in the world on your behalf)

**Ch 11. Agency and the Three Structural Failures — *What Goes Wrong When the AI Acts in the World on Your Behalf*** *(22–28 pp)*
The publication's automated headline-generation agent, deployed to the social desk, pushes a draft headline to Twitter/X 47 minutes before any human reviews. The headline misrepresents a quoted source. Reach: 230,000 impressions before takedown. Vendor-agnostic definition of an AI agent. Three structural failures: no stakeholder model, no self-model, no private deliberation surface. Blast radius for Agency along four dimensions (reach, reversibility, identity verification, escalation).

> **Portfolio artifact — Agentic Blast-Radius Decision Aid.** Layer A template + Layer B application, framework. Four-dimension analysis applied to one named or hypothesized agentic system in the reader's field. The reader's `DESIGN.md` is updated here with stakeholder-model and self-model criteria.

**Ch 12. Verification and Diligence Under Autonomy — *The Human Decision Node and Adversarial Validation*** *(32–40 pp; densest chapter)*
Priya at 2:14 PM playing human-in-the-loop on the publication's AI moderation agent. The agent has flagged a comment as harassment from what looks like a recurring bad actor. Three options: accept, reject, discern. The chapter teaches Option 3 under time pressure. Adversarial validation as documented spiral return of the four moves from Ch 5. Four diagnostic questions at the Human Decision Node (authority, information, time, accountability). Three obscuring mechanisms reweighted for Agency. Complete worked example: a proposed agentic deployment evaluated end-to-end.

> **Portfolio artifact — Human-in-the-Loop Protocol.** Layer A template + Layer B customization, framework. Four-condition test customized to the reader's authority and stakes.

**Ch 13. The Fluent Practitioner — *Where the Practice Goes From Here*** *(14–18 pp)*
Priya, now two years post-Ch 0, eighteen months post-Ch 8. Two questions: where were you with AI work the day you started this book? Where are you now? Re-assessment on the four-level scale. Deliberate practice for fluency. Fluency in your domain. The 90-day development plan. The closing question.

> **Portfolio artifact — Final Audit + 90-Day Practice Plan + Portfolio Cover Memo.** Layer A synthesis + Layer B paired with Ch 0, synthesis. The Ch 0 baseline audit is redone on the same piece of past work; the deliberate-practice plan is set; the one-page Cover Memo is drafted addressed to a future reader who has never heard of this book. **The Cover Memo is the most important single artifact in the portfolio — it is the cover letter every future reader sees first.**

## Appendices

**Appendix A. Pattern Reference** *(12–16 pp)*
Practical-core catalogue (~8 patterns) with one-page-each treatments and a decision tree for pattern-by-Loop-step selection. Cross-link to *How to Speak Bot* for the full catalogue.

**Appendix B. The Seven Tiers Full Taxonomy** *(10–14 pp)*
One page per Tier with diagnostic questions and common mis-classifications. Two worksheets — the four-question delegation defense and the blast-radius assessment.

## Translate-before-you-move-on

Every chapter ends with one explicit translation prompt the reader has to do, not read. *"Priya's failure was a journalism artifact — a fabricated source. In your field, what's the equivalent? Name the specific artifact that, in your work, would be the load-bearing claim a fabricated AI output could take down. Don't move to Ch 1 until you've named it."* The prompt produces the chapter's portfolio artifact in the reader's domain. Translation step and portfolio production are the same activity, named once.
